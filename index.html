About

I work at the intersection of simulation and hardware — developing thermal models, then validating them against physical prototypes. My career spans early-stage energy companies where I have owned projects from concept through commissioning.

My experience includes molten salt thermal energy storage systems, nuclear hardware, data center cooling infrastructure, and high-temperature industrial heat pumps. I am comfortable in both the design office and on the shop floor.

I use Python, MATLAB, ANSYS Fluent, and Excel-based heat and mass balances as primary tools. I hold GD&T proficiency for high-temperature rotating and static machinery.
